Identity Standards

Updated 28 September 2018

The Identity Standards tell you the best way to check someone’s identity.

The Identity Standards are made up of several pieces of guidance. If you follow the guidance, your service will be consistent with other services that have met the Identity Standards.

If you follow the Identity Standards, you can be more sure your users:

  • are who they say they are
  • only get access to things they're meant to have

Following the Identity Standards can also:

  • lower risks, such as identity theft, to your service and your users
  • let you get fraud and threat information from other organisations

Many existing identity checking processes are built to meet the Identity Standards. You can use one of these processes or follow the Identity Standards to build your own way to check identity.

The Identity Standards are also designed to be compatible with existing regulations, such as the eIDAS regulation.

Choose how you check identities

The Identity Standards explain the different methods you can use to check someone’s identity. You can pick and choose the methods that are best for your service.

How confident you can be that someone is who they say they are depends on how thoroughly you choose to carry out a check. If you need to be really certain of the person's identity, the Identity Standards will tell you what you need to do.
